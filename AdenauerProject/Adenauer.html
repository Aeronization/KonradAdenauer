Konrad Adenauer

This is the man who picked up the pieces of Germany after the fall of Adolf Hitler. He was the first elected Chancellor of West Germany.

Here is a timeline of Chancellor Adenauer's Life.

    1876: Born in Cologne, German.
    1894: Completes schooling and begins studying law.
    1896: Conscripted into Germany Army, but did not pass physical exams due to chronic respiratory problems.
    1906: Is elected to Cologne's city council.
    1917-1933: Elected as Mayor of Cologne. He worked to keep Cologne profitable and safe during a period of turbulence.
    1945: Konrad Adenauer helps found the Christian Democratic Union of Germany.
    1949-1963: Elected as Chancellor of West Germany.
    1955: Élysée Treaty which ended centuries of hostility with France and increasing trade between France and West Germany.
    1967: Dies at the age of 91.

Konrad Adenaur lived a life of service. He spent much of his life focusing on keeping his hometown of Cologne safe and secure during both World War I and World War II. However, after the conclusion of World War II and founding of the CDU, Adenauer spent the rest of his life trying to find peaceful and sound solutions to many diverse issues facing West Germany as Chancellor. He laid the framework for the reunification of West and East Germany in 1990. Konrad Adenauer was one of the most influential and important politicians in recent Germany history.
